LIFT (2024)

Jan 5th 2024 5 Min read

Lift: A Story of Redemption

The morally complex drama revolves around a heist gone wrong, which is very interesting as it helps the audience to rethink some choices they have made in life. The whole movie was supposed to give the audience a glimpse into the life of a criminal, and while it does do this, it manages to tell the story of self forgiveness as well.

While the characters seem to deal with a lot of internal conflict, the film deals with complex topics like trust, betrayal and forgiveness. This makes the audience able to relate to the powerful message of the movie which is self redemption. This is what distinguishes “lift” from the other films in the same genre. The anticipation and investment in the emotions of the characters never diminishes regardless of how much action there is.

Even the editor of lift managed to maintain tension and suspense throughout the movie. This, along with Joone’s incredible direction of the film allows the audience to slowly uncover the true motives of the characters and have their emotional expectations fulfilled.

Everything in Lift is so well made, including the performances, with the actors deeply capturing the character of a person grapples with his flaws and seeks forgiveness. The film is particularly adept at asking the audience what they would do in the same circumstances as the characters, all the while delivering a suspenseful story full of surprising twists. It’s a fascinating thriller that manages to bring both legs of the genre to the table.
